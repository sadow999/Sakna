The Story Begins

A Question Born at Dabaa

In 2022, I was a Project Engineer on one of Africa's largest active infrastructure programs — the El Dabaa Nuclear Power Plant on Egypt's Mediterranean coast. Managing water supply networks for 174+ buildings, coordinating with multinational stakeholders, enforcing nuclear QA/QC standards.

But every day, working next to the sea, in a basin with deep ecological and human history, the questions that had always lived at the edge of my attention came into sharp focus: who owns these water rights? What happens to the environmental integrity of this basin?

Supported by managers who saw the same tensions, I began researching. That inquiry led me from construction sites to the corridors of IHE Delft — and eventually to UNESCO, where I found the language, the community, and the mandate to pursue what had been quietly building: the intersection of technical rigor and cultural stewardship of water.

Coastal · Blue Carbon · Red Sea

Green Economy & Mangroves

Strengthening Coastal Resilience & Blue Carbon · Wadi El Gemal & Hamata · 2026–2028
€500,000 · 24 Months

A devastating 30% decline in mangrove cover driven by urbanization, camel overgrazing, and climate change. Past initiatives failed by planting in wrong sediment depths or ignoring local hydrology. We must stop treating mangroves as 'nature to be conserved' and start treating them as the foundation of a thriving Blue Carbon Economy.

By merging 40 years of Egyptian Space Agency satellite data (1985–2025) with the Traditional Ecological Knowledge of the Ababda Bedouin Tribes — we direct planting with surgical precision. Four pillars: Precision Ecological Restoration (20,000 trees, 2 nurseries, 20km coastline), Protection Infrastructure (camel-proof fencing, tidal channel restoration), Green Skills TVET for 60 local youth and women, and Continuous NDVI Satellite Monitoring.

Publications & Policy Work · Arab MAB Programme

Drylands, Oases &
Heritage-Based Solutions

A series of publications on Man and the Biosphere and Drylands in the Arab World establishes a paradigm shift: oases are not merely natural systems requiring technical interventions. They are heritage-based solutions where cultural practices, traditional knowledge, and social structures are integral to ecological resilience.

The core insight: linguistic diversity in oasis communities directly correlates with agrobiodiversity. The Siwi Amazigh language contains precise terminology for traditional cultivation and water management that has maintained date palm genetic diversity for centuries. When the language erodes, the ecosystem erodes.

This intellectual framework — developed through field missions to Fayoum University, Siwa Oasis, and New Valley Governorate — underpins the AECID proposals, the WEFE competition, and the 12-country Expert Group meeting on dryland groundwater management (UNESCO Cairo, June 2025).

Urban Heritage Framework · UNESCO / MoTA · Historic Cairo

University Heritage Forum:
A Living Laboratory

Launched July 2023, the UHF is a pioneering initiative positioned as a "living laboratory" for Historic Cairo — a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979. Over 85 participants from 8 universities and 3 public institutions produced 7 detailed technical reports, directly informing Historic Cairo's Conservation and Management Plan in alignment with UNESCO's Historic Urban Landscape Recommendation and Egypt Vision 2030.

Theme 01

Zero-Energy Cities — Citadel Area

A cutting-edge Digital Twin of the Mohamed Ali Mosque was developed using Autodesk Revit and Unreal Engine, enabling predictive maintenance and solar energy simulations.

Key Output: A replicable "Zero-Energy City" model for heritage sites in dense urban contexts.
Theme 02

Green Infrastructure — Fustat

Proposals for urban agriculture, water-sensitive design, rooftop gardens, and reintroduction of historically relevant plant species through participatory landscape planning.

Key Output: A community-managed green space framework restoring Fustat's agricultural heritage.
Theme 03

Cultural Economy — El-Gamaliya

Immersive heritage trails, creative economy clusters, and a proposed visitor center in Muhib El-Din Hall. Strategic linking of traditional crafts with cultural tourism.

Key Output: A Heritage Economic Activation Plan converting intangible assets into sustainable income.
Theme 04

Urban Finance — Darb Al-Ahmar

Strategies for Khayer Bek (dark tourism VR experiences, artisan market) and Bayt Al-Razzaz (boutique hotel). Innovative financing mechanisms including microfinance schemes.

Key Output: A Creative Financing Toolkit bridging private investment with heritage conservation.

Research & Publications

The Zankalon Forecast:
Machine Learning & The Nile Delta

MSc Thesis — Under Review for Publication · 2026

"Assessing the Impact of Climate Change on Agricultural Water Consumption Using Remote Sensing Data and Machine Learning: A Case Study of Zankalon, Nile Delta, Egypt"

🛰

Data: WaPOR v2 (30m AETI), AgERA5 reanalysis, ensemble of 23 CMIP6 GCMs under SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5 through 2099.

🤖

Method: Nine ML algorithms evaluated. Best performers: Random Forest (Orchards R²=0.863, Wheat R²=0.684), SVR (Rice R²=0.832), Ridge (Clover R²=0.770).

📊

Policy Finding: Agricultural expansion drives +13.5% CWR increase by 2050—substantially exceeding the 3.6% from climate change alone. Land-use policy is the critical lever.
